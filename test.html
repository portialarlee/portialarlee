3. choosing to live in desolate, confusing, and vibrant worlds

[a bunch of fragments of thoughts about these topics from other better thinkers:]

"preguntando caminamos"

“We had been in the world for a while, image-wounded, image-lit, we talked across the tracks, we had reasons for staying, we were hunched in the metro within the great circulation, red posters hung on the walls, they seemed to announce further defeats in cryptic terms, the main feeling on the platform was hardly a feeling at all, the sensation of the circuits routing through us, it was the way of being in the world we had been offered, and not being able to name it was part of this, when we hung posters they were world posters, the images hurt or betrayed us and we did the same to them, finally we lived together in the image-world.” - from Red Posters by Joshua Clover

"whenever the incorruptible mother boats come back to you they return by the rivers where you can never wash twice" -from Mantra by Maung Thein Zaw

"Everything we write will be used against us. Every claim on or lament against society that we write will be received in the same way as accounts of rape—as lies. We don't care anymore. As soon as we stop resisting the charge we can turn around and face the others that have not accused us, those we should have been talking to the whole time. We name this journal after the shame we no longer feel and commemorate all these outcast comrades: the witches, crones, hysterics, spinsters, she-wolves, oracles, and misfits—our fellow travellers.

See you at the gun club / dance hall / hot springs / savage blockade."

Portia’s rationale on why friends are good: “Because then you get to live in all of their worlds as well your own.”

“Adorno: Oh Habermas my dear dear friend. The protesters again! Oh they kill me slowly. ….

A voice comes from amongst the sea of students….

Student 1: YOU SIR ARE A WAGNER

Student 2: GOD IS DEAD, LET US ATTACK THOSE WHO HAVE REPLACED HIM WITH CHAREST

Student 3: YOU CALL US LEFT WING FASCISTS? BUT IT IS YOUR POLICE STATE THAT BEATS US DOWN IN THE STREET

Student 4: ADORNO YOU ARE AN INSTITUTION AND AS AN INSTITUION, YOU ARE DEAD.”

"But before we left, we forgot to do something We forgot to burn the city The city of suffering We burnt the city and we went underground."
