1. guinea pigs & revolutionary insurrection

The Wise Guinea Pig approached and with a nonsense smile goofily spoke the following words:

Dear Portia,

Today is the day. Well, no, it is not the day, because no day is the day. I guess what I meant to say is that today is well, the only day, and so I do hope you move through it like a hamster in a caged wheel of life. A wheel of fortune. My dearest dear. Meaning, please linger where it is needed and please move like a spring chicken where it is needed. And please also rest when your sacred heart says I am tired. Because, the thing about it being the only day is that there is quite potentially a window of time in which you may just spin and spin and spin that dollar store wheel (while all your friends spin and spin their wheels) until the cage is no longer such a cage. For the interesting thing about those wheels you all purchased is that they turn into something else quite suddenly when a collective movement resonates through the inbetween spaces of their metallic structures. And it has something to do with it being a moment of quite a number of histories that you my dear are a part of…. I bid you luck my dearest dear.

With love,

The Guinea Pig (who cannot be named)

9. The Rankin Family and Gilmore Girls

One time Portia visited my home state. It is the state (of being) which I felt had shaped my existence, the paradigms by which I shape my thought. I was happy and proud to tour her around the place. Home being, at least for me, one of the most important things. She, along with our friend Christina, seemed primarily concerned with locating the town centers, buildings, and local businesses that would most closely resemble those from Gilmore Girls, which she told me repeatedly was a carbon copy or at least a facsimile of my life. “This is my favorite record store,” I might say. “Ah yes, this is where [character] would go,” she might reply. “You are such a [so-and-so character],” it would continue.

On Gilmore Girls, Nan, and Anarchists on the Television: “We should strive to have the ability to speak in multiple languages. It is worthwhile to abandon your default political discourse and attempt to mobilize someone who has been reading Margaret Wente all summer. Unfortunately, during the height of the student movement, I spent a lot of time refuting my grandma's recollection of images of ‘violent anarchists’ she absorbed on mainstream news. … as a result of my involvement in the strike, my grandma cannot differentiate between her granddaughter and the ‘violent anarchists’ she sees on TV. Over the dinner table, we try to bridge the gap between the terminology of various activist communities and her individualistic subjectivity.These relationships humanize the movement for the outsider. We break through mainstream media constructions of the event by sharing lived political experience. Through this relationship, the viewer can perceive these politically loaded televised images within a new framework. Action, anger, movement, frustration, structural oppression, which may have existed as abstract concepts to them will become real or at least, may elicit some association. The disconnected media consumer interacts with the greater movement through knowledge transferred within an intimate relationship.
